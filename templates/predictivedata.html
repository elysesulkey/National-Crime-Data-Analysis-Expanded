Population Forecast & Data Analysis

Analysis

Our intention for this research to examine if States containing cities with higher crime rates would see shrinking populations over the next decade, while states with lower crime rates would see rising populations.

After thorough analysis of our population predictions for 2028, we were unable to find a conclusive correlation between Crime Rates and Population Density. Our population predictions show noticeable increases in New Mexico and Nevada and a noticeable decrease in Kansas.

Additionally, we acknowledge that our dataset does not account for other factors that would affect a stateâ€™s population such as evolving job markets, tax incentives, birth rates and Counter-urbanization.

While researching other possible causes for population shifts, we found that unemployment was one of the leading causes for changes in population. Other leading factors include tax rates and cost of living.

Conclusion

We determined that crime statistics have a very minor impact on overall state populations. Our findings suggest that additional data variables will be required to more accurately identify the causes of population changes in future models.
